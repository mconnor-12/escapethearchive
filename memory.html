Your task — Prologue

You have been granted access to a restricted archival collection. Before proceeding, you must complete the intake protocol: annotate the exhibit brief, examine three document hotspots, and acknowledge any misalignments you discover.

Exhibit BriefRESTRICTED — Level 2 AccessArchive Reference: ETA-2024-001
Preliminary Exhibit Packet: "Comprehensive Account of the 1943 Administrative Relocation"

This exhibit presents a comprehensive account of the administrative relocation procedures implemented during the 1943 wartime period. All records have been reviewed for accuracy and completeness. The archive certifies that the materials included represent the full documentary record available for this period.

Sources include: official correspondence, administrative memoranda, photographic documentation, and government-certified witness accounts. Materials have been organized chronologically. Gaps in the record reflect voluntary non-participation by certain individuals who chose not to provide statements.

The exhibit aims to present a neutral, comprehensive, and definitive account of this historical period for educational and research purposes.

Auditor finding: "Voluntary non-participation" obscures coercive context. Individuals who did not provide statements may have been unable to — due to incarceration, displacement, or fear of reprisal. The framing attributes absence to individual choice rather than structural conditions.
Auditor finding: "Comprehensive" is a claim, not a description. Archives are never comprehensive — they are curated. The assertion of completeness may discourage future researchers from looking for what's missing.
Auditor finding: "Administrative relocation" is a euphemism. The historical record elsewhere names this as forced removal, incarceration, or internment. Language choice is not neutral — it shapes what the reader understands happened.
