Your task — Prologue: The Intake Desk

You have been granted access to a restricted archival collection. Before proceeding, you must complete the intake protocol: annotate the exhibit brief, examine three document hotspots, and acknowledge the misalignments you discover.

Exhibit Brief RESTRICTED — Level 2 Access Archive Reference: ETA-2024-001
Preliminary Exhibit Packet: "Comprehensive Account of the 1943 Administrative Relocation"

This exhibit presents a comprehensive account of the administrative relocation procedures implemented during the 1943 wartime period. All records have been reviewed for accuracy and completeness. The archive certifies that the materials included represent the full documentary record available for this period.

Sources include: official correspondence, administrative memoranda, photographic documentation, and government-certified witness accounts. Materials have been organized chronologically. Gaps in the record reflect voluntary non-participation by certain individuals who chose not to provide statements.

The exhibit aims to present a neutral, comprehensive account of this historical period. This is an administrative relocation record compiled for educational and research purposes.

🔎 Examine each highlighted phrase below — click or press Enter on all three to continue

Auditor finding — "voluntary non-participation": This phrase attributes the absence of testimony to individual choice rather than structural coercion. Individuals who did not provide statements may have been unable to — due to incarceration, displacement, fear of reprisal, or lack of access to the process. The framing conceals the conditions that shaped participation.
Auditor finding — "comprehensive account": "Comprehensive" is a claim, not a description. Archives are never comprehensive — they are curated. The assertion of completeness discourages future researchers from looking for what's missing. Comprehensiveness is a rhetorical posture, not an empirical fact.
Auditor finding — "administrative relocation": This is a euphemism. The historical record elsewhere names this as forced removal, incarceration, or internment. The WRA's own style guide directed staff to use institutional language to minimize the coercive character of the program. Language choice is not neutral — it shapes what the reader understands happened.

Transfer Processing — Administrative Summary
All transfers were conducted in accordance with Executive Order 9066. Processing centers reported no significant incidents. Property Disposition — Note to File
Property disposal proceeded under standard provisions. Records indicate that in cases where storage was unavailable, property was disposed of by local authorities. Itemized records were not required for disposals under $50 in value. Documentation is incomplete for the March–April period due to administrative transition.
